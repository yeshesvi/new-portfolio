Bringing User Centered Design to the government

Anonymous Tipping Platform for Jackson Police Department

As a part of the pilot design team, I have worked to bring the user-centered design principles we learn in class to the city governments. Our project created a texting platform for the police department to receive anonymous tips from any texting capable phone.

  • Researched and Surveyed a lot of local groups like NAACP, Churchs, Recreation centers and Citizens.
  • Through research we have found the need for an electronic tipping platform with a guarantee for anonymity
  • The solution we have designed is cloud based and very cost effective, it would cost nothing to the citizens and around $100 per month to the Police.
  • The 7988-JPD vanity number is registered for the citizens of Jackson to text tips to the police.

Reducing the number of support tickets by making users self reliant

Improving the self-help pages of A2Hosting resulting in a 40% decrease in the number of basic support requests.

The project came into existence as a reaction to the exponential growth in the sheer number of support requests being fielded by the customer support team of A2Hosting. Upon examination it was found that solutions to most of the tickets that were being submitted are already publicly available in A2Hosting's support documentation.

So, we were brought in to research the interaction and visibility issues of the website and provide clear achievable recommendations to fix them.
During the course of the project, as a five person team we have,

  • Created an interaction map (linked above) to understand the navigation of the support website
  • Conducted user intervies and designed targeted surveys to create personas, scenarios and story boards. We have used these resources to guide us throughout the rest of the process.
  • Performed a comparative analysis of support documentation provided by the immediate competitors of A2Hosting to understant the market landscape
    Comparative Analysis Report
  • Analysed the heuristics of the website to find common usability issues.
    Heuristic Evaluation Report
  • Conducted Usability tests to confirm out findings and submitted a findings report with a presentation to the stakeholders along with recommendations
    Usability Analysis Report
